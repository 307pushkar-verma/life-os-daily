😴 How to Sleep Better

1. Keep a consistent schedule — go to bed and wake up at the same time every day, even weekends. This anchors your circadian rhythm.

2. Create a wind-down ritual — 30 minutes before bed: dim lights, no screens, light stretching or reading a physical book.

3. Optimise your environment — room temperature 18–20°C, blackout curtains, white noise if needed. Your bedroom should be a sleep sanctuary.

4. Avoid stimulants — no caffeine after 2 pm, no heavy meals within 2 hours of sleep. Alcohol disrupts REM sleep even if it helps you fall asleep.

5. Get morning sunlight — 10–15 minutes of natural light within an hour of waking resets your sleep-wake clock for that night.

💪 How to Maintain Fitness

1. Consistency over intensity — exercising 5 days a week at moderate intensity beats sporadic all-out sessions. Show up, even on low-energy days.

2. The 3-pillar framework — combine strength training (2–3x/week), cardio (150 min/week), and flexibility work (daily 10 min) for complete fitness.

3. Progressive overload — increase weight, reps, or duration by 5–10% every week to keep making gains. Your body adapts — keep challenging it.

4. Recovery is training — sleep 7–9 hours, take rest days, and include active recovery (walking, yoga). Muscles grow during rest, not during workouts.

5. Track to stay accountable — log workouts, note weights used, and take monthly photos. Data reveals progress that the mirror doesn't always show.

💊 Supplements for Better Health & Fitness

🔵 Creatine Monohydrate (5g/day) — the most researched supplement in existence. Improves strength, power, and muscle mass. Take it daily, timing doesn't matter.

🟡 Vitamin D3 + K2 (2000–5000 IU) — most people are deficient, especially indoors. Critical for immunity, mood, testosterone, and bone health. Take with fat.

🟠 Omega-3 Fish Oil (2–3g EPA+DHA) — reduces inflammation, supports heart and brain health. Look for triglyceride form for better absorption.

🟢 Magnesium Glycinate (300–400mg, bedtime) — helps with sleep quality, stress regulation, muscle recovery, and hundreds of enzymatic reactions.

⚪ Protein (whey/plant) — if diet is short on protein, supplement to reach 1.6–2.2g per kg bodyweight. Crucial for muscle repair and satiety.

Always consult a doctor before starting supplements. The above are commonly safe for healthy adults.
